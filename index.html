Questions

Things parents actually ask us

No jargon, no runaround. Just clear answers.

Does my baby need to wear anything?

No. Nonnanest uses passive infrared sensors to read your baby's skin temperature from up to 4 feet away. Nothing touches your baby — no socks, no ankle bands, no clips, no straps. Mount the camera directly above the crib and it does the rest. Every other monitor that tracks skin temperature requires a wearable — Nonnanest is the only one that doesn't.

Does Nonnanest need WiFi?

No. Nonnanest communicates directly between the nursery camera and parent handheld using FHSS (Frequency Hopping Spread Spectrum) encryption. No WiFi, no cloud, no internet dependency. Private by design, not by promise.

How is Nonnanest different from wearable monitors?

Every other monitor that tracks skin temperature requires your baby to wear a sock or ankle band. Nonnanest is the only monitor that senses skin temperature contactlessly — nothing touches your baby. We also don't require WiFi, don't need an app or phone, and have no subscriptions. We're built for parents who want skin temperature awareness without putting anything on their baby.

How accurate is the skin temperature reading?

Nonnanest provides skin temperature awareness within ±1°F when mounted directly above the crib in pediatrician-recommended room temperatures (68–72°F). It tracks skin temperature trends continuously — giving you awareness of comfort shifts before they'd otherwise be obvious.

Is there a monthly subscription?

No. Nonnanest is a one-time purchase of $329. All features — skin temperature awareness, video monitoring, two-way audio, night vision, lullabies, room climate tracking — are included. No accounts, no tiers, no recurring fees.

Is Nonnanest a medical device?

No. Nonnanest is not a medical device. It is not intended to monitor vital signs or to diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure any disease or health condition. It provides skin temperature wellness insights to help parents stay aware of their baby's comfort and environment. For any health concerns, always consult a pediatrician.
